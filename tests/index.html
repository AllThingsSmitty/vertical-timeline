Vertical timeline ↓

  • 1946 Steven Spielberg is born in Cincinnati, Ohio to Leah Posner and Arnold Spielberg.
  • 1963 At age 16, Spielberg wrote and directed his first independent film, a 140-minute science fiction adventure called Firelight, which would later inspire Close Encounters.
  • 1965 Steven graduated from Saratoga High School in Saratoga, California. Soon after he graduated, Spielberg moved to Los Angeles.
  • 1969 Steven's first professional TV job came when he was hired to direct one of the segments for the pilot episode of Night Gallery.
  • 1971 Spielbeg's TV movie, Duel, based Richard Matheson adaptation is about a psychotic truck driver who chases the terrified driver.
  • 1975 Jaws is released with the tagline, "Just when you thought it was safe to go back into the water" and becomes the top-grossing film at that time.
  • 1977 Close Encounters of the Third Kind becomes a critical and box office hit, giving Spielberg his first Academy Award Best Director nomination.
  • 1981 Spielberg directs Harrison Ford in Raiders of teh Lost Ark based on friend George Lucas's story.
  • 1982 Spielberg returned to the science fiction genre with E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial which went on to become the top-grossing film of all time.
  • 1993 Jurassic Park: hold onto your butts.
  • 1993 Schindler's List
  • 2003 Catch Me if You Can
  • 2004 The Terminal
